Okapi

Their distinctly large ears help them to sense hidden predators. Their dark bodies blend into the shadows!.

  • Scientific Name: Okapia johnstoni.
  • Living Place: Democratic Republic of Congo.
  • Tongue Length: 12 to 14 inches.
  • Language: Coughs, bleats, and whistles.

The okapi may look like a zebra due to the stripes on its legs, but it is actually more closely related to the giraffe. Okapis belong to the family Giraffidae, and they are the only living relatives of giraffes. Unlike giraffes, okapis are shorter and adapted to life in dense rainforests.
